Milestones That Changed Healthcare

From ancient remedies to mRNA vaccines — the pivotal discoveries that built modern medicine.

1628

William Harvey — Blood Circulation

Harvey demonstrated that blood circulates continuously through the body pumped by the heart, overthrowing 1,400 years of Galenic medicine and founding modern physiology.

PHYSIOLOGY
1796

Edward Jenner — The First Vaccine

Jenner showed that inoculation with cowpox protected against smallpox, creating the world's first vaccine and launching the field of immunology that would eventually eradicate smallpox.

IMMUNOLOGY
1928

Alexander Fleming — Penicillin

A contaminated petri dish led to the discovery that Penicillium mould inhibits bacteria. The subsequent development of penicillin antibiotics is estimated to have saved over 200 million lives.

MICROBIOLOGY
1953

Watson & Crick — The DNA Double Helix

The discovery of DNA's structure explained how genetic information is stored and copied — arguably the most important scientific finding of the 20th century, launching molecular biology.

GENETICS
2003

Human Genome Project Completed

After 13 years of international collaboration, the full sequence of human DNA — 3.2 billion base pairs — was mapped, opening the era of personalised and genomic medicine.

GENOMICS
2020

mRNA Vaccines — A New Platform

The pandemic accelerated decades of research, producing the first approved mRNA vaccines in under a year. This platform is now being developed for cancer, HIV, and influenza treatments.
